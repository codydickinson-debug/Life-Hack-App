Privacy Policy

Ascend — a local-first habit, goal, and money tracker

✓ No accounts   ✓ No tracking   ✓ Encrypted at rest

Ascend is built around one principle: your data stays on your device unless you explicitly opt into a cloud feature. This page explains exactly what's stored, what (if anything) leaves your device, and what choice you have. For the technical detail of how data is protected end-to-end, see the Security Overview.

The short version. Habits, goals, transactions, wins, mood, notes, and net-worth history live in your browser's local storage. There's no account to sign up for, no analytics, no ad networks, no cookies for tracking. Three optional features call third parties — bank sync (Plaid), AI counselor (Anthropic), and stock quotes (Yahoo Finance) — and only when you opt in. Each is described below.

1. What's stored on your device

Everything you enter into Ascend is written to your browser's localStorage under the ascend_v2 key. That includes:

  • Habits, streaks, daily completions, and pause history
  • Goals, plans, and step lists
  • Manual transactions, savings buckets, recurring bills, manual assets and liabilities
  • Wins, mood entries, calendar notes, reflections
  • Net-worth history (90-day rolling)
  • Settings (theme, accent color, hidden tab/section preferences, Cornileus chat history if not auto-cleared)
  • If you connect a bank: account balances, account names, transactions, and an item identifier (no credentials)

This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly trigger an action that requires it (Export, AI counselor message, bank sync).

Encryption at rest (optional)

You can set a passphrase in Settings to AES-GCM encrypt your local data. The key is derived via PBKDF2-SHA256 (600,000 iterations) and held only in memory. If you forget the passphrase, your data is unrecoverable by design — there is no backdoor. Auto-lock can re-prompt for the passphrase after a configurable idle period.

2. What leaves your device (only if you opt in)

2.1 Plaid bank sync

If you tap "Connect a bank" and complete the flow, Ascend talks to a Cloudflare Worker backend that Ascend operates, and to Plaid. Your use of Plaid through Ascend is also governed by Plaid's End User Privacy Policy, which we recommend reading.

  • Sent to Plaid: the bank account credentials you enter into Plaid Link (these are never seen by Ascend or by the backend — they go directly to Plaid).
  • Sent to Ascend's backend: a per-device user identifier and the Plaid public token, which is exchanged for an access token. The access token is AES-GCM encrypted with a server-side key before being written to Cloudflare KV. Each device enrolls separately and receives its own credentials — no other Ascend user can read your tokens or transactions.
  • Synced to your device: account balances, transactions, and account metadata. Stored only on your device.

Plaid products used. Ascend uses the following Plaid products to power its features. Each is only invoked when you trigger the corresponding action in the app:

  • Transactions — recent transaction history and categorization (Money tab, spend analytics).
  • Auth — account and routing numbers for the bank accounts you connect (account list, balances).
  • Identity — name on the account, for matching deposits to paychecks (Money tab paycheck detection).
  • Liabilities — APR, balance, and minimum payment on credit cards, student loans, and mortgages (debt payoff planner).
  • Investments (Holdings + Investment Transactions) — portfolio positions and trades (Stocks tab integration with linked brokerage accounts).

Data retention. Your encrypted access token is retained in Cloudflare KV for as long as you have an active bank connection. When you disconnect a bank (Settings → Bank sync → Disconnect), the encrypted token is removed from the backend within minutes and we instruct Plaid to revoke the underlying item. If your device has had no activity for 12 consecutive months, we may proactively delete your enrolled device record and any associated encrypted tokens; you can re-enroll at any time. Transaction data synced to your device lives only on your device — clearing browser data, uninstalling, or tapping Settings → "Reset everything" erases it locally with no server-side copy to recover.

You can disconnect a bank at any time from Settings → Bank sync, which removes the encrypted token from the backend immediately. You can also opt to self-host the Worker (Settings → "Use a custom backend") so tokens live on infrastructure you fully control — same code, your servers.

Webhook signature verification. When Plaid notifies Ascend's backend of an item event (re-auth needed, permission revoked, etc.), every webhook is signed by Plaid with an ES256 JWT. The backend verifies the signature against Plaid's published key set, checks that the body hash matches the signed claim, and enforces a 5-minute replay window before accepting the event. Unverified or replayed events are rejected — no in-app "reconnect your bank" prompt can be spoofed.

2.2 Cornileus AI counselor

When you send a message to Cornileus, Ascend forwards your message plus a snapshot of your financial situation (recent transactions, savings balances, debts, goals — capped at ~18KB) to a backend AI proxy. The proxy attaches an Anthropic API key (which lives only on the backend, never on your device) and forwards the request to Anthropic.

  • Anthropic processes the message to generate a response. Per their API terms, inputs and outputs aren't used to train their models.
  • The chat thread is held in your local storage and auto-cleared when you close the counselor sheet (you can disable auto-clear).
  • Per-user daily call cap (default 50) is enforced server-side to bound runaway costs.

2.3 Stock quotes & market data

The Stocks tab fetches live quotes, market scans, and news headlines from a Python backend that proxies Yahoo Finance. Only the tickers you query are sent — no personal information.

4. What we never collect

  • No analytics or telemetry of any kind. No Google Analytics, no Mixpanel, no PostHog, no Sentry.
  • No ad networks, no third-party tracking pixels, no Meta/TikTok pixels.
  • No location data.
  • No contacts, photos, or device identifiers (other than the per-device random ID Ascend generates locally to talk to your backend).
  • No cookies for tracking. The site uses no cookies at all.

5. Your controls

  • Privacy mode (eye icon, top-right) blurs every dollar amount across the app for screen-sharing.
  • Encryption (Settings → Privacy & encryption) AES-encrypts everything at rest behind a passphrase.
  • Auto-lock re-prompts for your passphrase after a configurable idle period.
  • Export data (Settings → Data) downloads a full JSON backup, plain or encrypted.
  • Reset everything (Settings → Data) erases all local data. This action cannot be undone.
  • Disconnect a bank (Settings → Bank sync) removes the encrypted access token from your backend.

6. Your privacy rights (CCPA, GDPR, and similar)

Because Ascend stores your data on your device by default, most data-subject rights are exercised directly through the in-app controls in §5 — you don't need to send us a request to access, correct, or delete data we don't hold. That said:

  • Right to know / access: tap Settings → Export data to download a complete JSON backup of everything Ascend stores about you.
  • Right to correct: every field in Ascend is editable in-app. There is no separate operator-held copy to correct.
  • Right to delete / erasure: tap Settings → Reset everything to erase local data. If you have a connected bank, also tap Settings → Bank sync → Disconnect to remove the encrypted access token from the backend. If you want any residual device-enrollment records purged, email the operator (§3) and they will be removed within 30 days.
  • Right to restrict processing: do not connect a bank, do not use Cornileus, and do not use the Stocks tab — Ascend will operate as a fully local app with no third-party calls.
  • Right to object to automated decision-making: Ascend does not make automated decisions about you in the GDPR sense. Cornileus produces educational responses; it does not classify, score, or take action on your behalf.
  • Right to portability: the JSON export is in a standard, machine-readable format.
  • Non-discrimination (CCPA): we do not change your access to Ascend based on whether you exercise these rights.

We do not sell personal information. We have no advertising, no data brokers, and no third-party analytics. There is no opt-out to set because there is no sale or share to opt out of.
