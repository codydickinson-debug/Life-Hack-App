Privacy Policy

Ascend — a local-first habit, goal, and money tracker

✓ No accounts   ✓ No tracking   ✓ Encrypted at rest

Ascend is built around one principle: your data stays on your device unless you explicitly opt into a cloud feature. This page explains exactly what's stored, what (if anything) leaves your device, and what choice you have.

The short version. Habits, goals, transactions, wins, mood, notes, and net-worth history live in your browser's local storage. There's no account to sign up for, no analytics, no ad networks, no cookies for tracking. Three optional features call third parties — bank sync (Plaid), AI counselor (Anthropic), and stock quotes (Yahoo Finance) — and only when you opt in. Each is described below.

1. What's stored on your device

Everything you enter into Ascend is written to your browser's localStorage under the ascend_v2 key. That includes:

  • Habits, streaks, daily completions, and pause history
  • Goals, plans, and step lists
  • Manual transactions, savings buckets, recurring bills, manual assets and liabilities
  • Wins, mood entries, calendar notes, reflections
  • Net-worth history (90-day rolling)
  • Settings (theme, accent color, hidden tab/section preferences, Cornileus chat history if not auto-cleared)
  • If you connect a bank: account balances, account names, transactions, and an item identifier (no credentials)

This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly trigger an action that requires it (Export, AI counselor message, bank sync).

Encryption at rest (optional)

You can set a passphrase in Settings to AES-GCM encrypt your local data. The key is derived via PBKDF2-SHA256 (600,000 iterations) and held only in memory. If you forget the passphrase, your data is unrecoverable by design — there is no backdoor. Auto-lock can re-prompt for the passphrase after a configurable idle period.

2. What leaves your device (only if you opt in)

2.1 Plaid bank sync

If you tap "Connect a bank" and complete the flow, Ascend talks to your own Cloudflare Worker backend (which you configured) and to Plaid:

  • Sent to Plaid: the bank account credentials you enter into Plaid Link (these are never seen by Ascend or by your backend — they go directly to Plaid).
  • Sent to your backend: a per-device user identifier and the Plaid public token, which is exchanged for an access token. The access token is AES-GCM encrypted with a key only your backend knows before being written to Cloudflare KV.
  • Synced to your device: account balances, transactions, and account metadata.

You can disconnect a bank at any time from Settings → Bank sync, which removes the encrypted token from your backend.

2.2 Cornileus AI counselor

When you send a message to Cornileus, Ascend forwards your message plus a snapshot of your financial situation (recent transactions, savings balances, debts, goals — capped at ~18KB) to a backend AI proxy. The proxy attaches an Anthropic API key (which lives only on the backend, never on your device) and forwards the request to Anthropic.

  • Anthropic processes the message to generate a response. Per their API terms, inputs and outputs aren't used to train their models.
  • The chat thread is held in your local storage and auto-cleared when you close the counselor sheet (you can disable auto-clear).
  • Per-user daily call cap (default 50) is enforced server-side to bound runaway costs.

2.3 Stock quotes & market data

The Stocks tab fetches live quotes, market scans, and news headlines from a Python backend that proxies Yahoo Finance. Only the tickers you query are sent — no personal information.

3. What we never collect

  • No analytics or telemetry of any kind. No Google Analytics, no Mixpanel, no PostHog, no Sentry.
  • No ad networks, no third-party tracking pixels, no Meta/TikTok pixels.
  • No location data.
  • No contacts, photos, or device identifiers (other than the per-device random ID Ascend generates locally to talk to your backend).
  • No cookies for tracking. The site uses no cookies at all.

4. Your controls

  • Privacy mode (eye icon, top-right) blurs every dollar amount across the app for screen-sharing.
  • Encryption (Settings → Privacy & encryption) AES-encrypts everything at rest behind a passphrase.
  • Auto-lock re-prompts for your passphrase after a configurable idle period.
  • Export data (Settings → Data) downloads a full JSON backup, plain or encrypted.
  • Reset everything (Settings → Data) erases all local data. This action cannot be undone.
  • Disconnect a bank (Settings → Bank sync) removes the encrypted access token from your backend.

5. Children

Ascend is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone (children or adults) — see "What we never collect" above.

6. Changes to this policy

If anything here changes, we'll update the date below and call out the change in the in-app "What's new" sheet on your next visit.
